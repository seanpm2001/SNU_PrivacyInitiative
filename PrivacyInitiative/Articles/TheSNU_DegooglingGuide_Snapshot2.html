Background on degoogling

Degoogling is a difficult process in 2020 due to Googles monopoly and ecosystem. I used to use Google heavily before late 2018. I used many different products, and after my turn for privacy, and new standards, I have gone about and worked on degoogling.

Even the first major versions of SNU were Google based unfortunately. I have successfully degoogled out of most products, the ones I personally find the most difficult to get rid of are Google Play, Android, and YouTube. I have abandoned my GMAIL, but unfortunately still have to use it sometimes.

The process of Degoogling is just taking Google out of your life. It is difficult, but hopefully someday it will be possible.

Alternates

Alternates to Google Drive

There aren't too many good alternates to Google Drive. If you are able to, you can set up your own file server.

If you want to jump ship and go to the Microsoft monopoly (like I had to do for now) you can try OneDrive.

OneDrive

Pros

* You have moved away from a Google product

* You have a working online file service

Cons

* You have entered/gone deeper into the Microsoft monopoly

* There is 10 gigabytes less free space available with free accounts on OneDrive than there is on Google Drive (OneDrive: 5GB, Google Drive: 15 GB)

* Your privacy is still at risk.

Alternates to YouTube

YouTube is the dominant video platform at the moment, and it is difficult to find alternates. There are some tools you can use to enhance YouTube, as most content creators refuse to leave.

Some tools include YouTube Vanced, which is a free client for YouTube that adds in premium features from YouTube Red/Premium such as built-in ad blocking, playing audio while the app is minimized, etc.

YouTube Vanced

YouTube Vanced has to be installed a specific way. Check out the YouTube Vanced subreddit for more details

Some popular alternates to YouTube include:

Dailymotion

DailyMotion is a popular video site, it contains lots of videos, but it doesn't have most YouTube content creators

Pros

* You have found 1 alternate from the most dominant Google Monopoly piece

Cons

* Content from many YouTube creators is unavailable here

* The privacy level of DailyMotion is uncertain

Vimeo

Vimeo is a very popular video site for professional videos, but it doesn't work well enough to rival YouTube. Due to its ad-free nature, it costs money to get weekly uploads, and there is a lack of content in many categories. It is still an alternate to YouTube though.

Pros

* It is an ad-free video site

* Vimeo contains many professional high quality videos

Cons

* it doesn't work well enough to rival YouTube

* It has a very minimal amount of content compared to YouTube

BitTube

BitTube is a video site that allows for the upload of videos. It is a rival to YouTube, and it even contains the option to import an entire YouTube channel.

Pros

* BitTube has the functionality to upload an entire YouTube channel to it

* It is an alternate video site to YouTube

Cons

* There is a 3 hour limit on video lenghth

* There aren't very many videos on the site compared to YouTube

* The privacy level of this site is uncertain

* The owner may not have sufficient servers to be able to rival YouTube in the long run

Alternates to Android

Android is currently the most popular operating system in the world. It has a massive 2,000,000,000+ (2 billion plus) user marketshare and is the dominant mobile operating system. Unfortunately, it is also a Google product.

The reason why a lot of people choose to stay with Android is because they don't want to get iOS and an iPhone/iPod/iPad. One of the reasons I switched back in 2014 was because my iPod 5 didn't have support for SD cards, and apple mobile devices at the time didn't have support for them (I think even today that is still the case, but I haven't checked recently)

There aren't many other alternates to Android. There are ways to root Android, and get a degoogled distribution. Here are some examples:

iOS/iPadOS

If you are able to switch to the Apple ecosystem, and pay the extra money for an iOS device, you can switch to iOS. Switching from Android to iOS is one of thrw options. Privacy is uncertain, as we can only take Apples word due to iOS being proprietary closed-source software.

Note: iOS currently cannot run on any Android devices, as it is not compatible, and an iOS Operating System file is not yet available

Pros

* You have moved away from the Android ecosystem, and another Google product

* iOS does a lot of the same stuff that Android does

* iOS devices have a much higher support range than Google devices. For example, an iPhone 5 is still not obsolete after 7 years and continues to receive updates from Apple

* iOS contains many apps that run on Android

Cons

* Privacy is uncertain, as Apple maintains iOS as closed source software. We can only take their word.

* Apple devices are typically a lot more expensive than the devices that run Android

* iOS lacks some features, such as SD card support, and is usually lacking behind by 1-6 years on most major Android features.

* iOS is hard to customize without rooting the device.

* iOS isn't compatible with all games that are on Android, a lot of them haven't been ported over

iPadOS

With the release of iPadOS, you now have multiple options for apple systems, one being exclusive to tablets, and the other for iPhones and iPods

iPadOS is very similar to iOS

Pros

* You have moved away from the Android ecosystem, and another Google product

* iPadOS does a lot of the same stuff that Android does

* iPadOS contains many apps that run on Android

Cons

* Privacy is uncertain, as Apple maintains iPadOS as closed source software. We can only take their word.

* Apple devices are typically a lot more expensive than the devices that run Android, especially tablets

* iPadOS lacks some features, such as SD card support, and is usually lacking behind by 1-6 years on most major Android features.

* iPadOS is hard to customize without rooting the device.

* iPadOS isn't compatible with all games that are on Android, a lot of them haven't been ported over

Alternates to Google Chrome

Google Chrome is currently the most commonly used browser in the world, and it is also part of Googles monopoly. Despite lacking many W3 standard features, and having other issues, it is constantly being pushed out. Google Chrome is an easy one to get rid of (as long as you don't have ChromeOS)

Personally, I use Firefox. It does nearly everything good that Chrome does, and it isn't as memory intensive, and it is a lot faster. Mozilla Firefox has nearly a half billion users as of February 9th 2020

Mozilla Firefox

Firefox is a free and open source web browser.

Pros

* Everything good that Chrome does works with Firefox

* You don't have to deal with the Google Chrome monopoly

* There are no known privacy issues with Firefox

* Firefox is a very stable browser

* Firefox is not memory intensive

* Firefox is open source, opposed to being a freeware application

* Firefox is actively updated

* Firefox source code goes all the way back to Netscape

Cons

* None that I can currently find

Safari

Safari is a closed-source proprietary browser by apple that uses the WebKit engine (what Chromium was based on) however as of Februar 10th 2020, it is Google-free

Pros

* Most good Chrome features work with Safari

* You moved away from another Google product

* Safari is a stable browser

* Safari supports some file types that Firefox doesn't

Cons

* The privacy of Safari is uncertain, we can only know what apple tells us

* Many HTML5 tags do not work with Safari

* There is no Windows, Linux, BSD, and Solaris support, only MacOS, and iOS

* Safari is proprietary and receives updates rarely

DuckDuckGo browser

The DuckDuckGo browser is a browser for Android and iOS that lets the user browse the web with DuckDuckGo. It is a basic browser, and it has the functionality of deleting the current search history with 1 tap

Pros

* Browse the web exclusively with DuckDuckGo

* Clear your search history with the click of a single button

* Basic web brower functionality

Cons

* No support for Windows, MacOS, Linux [source needed]

SeaMonkey browser

Pros

* It is a full-on web suite

* It has full web browsing capability

Cons

* None that I can currently find

Google search alternates

Googles search engine is what defines Google. It isn't that hard to get away from. There are many good alternate search engines that have the same functionality as Google, and focus on privacy

DuckDuckGo

DuckDuckGo (DDG) is a privacy oriented search engine that rivals Google search. It contains high functionality, and crawls the web similarly to Google. It also uses Bing serch results

Pros

* DDG has high search functionality

* DDG can browse the deep web

* DDG works in most web browsers

* DDG is heavily committed to user privacy

* DDG has a very good unit converting system

* DDG displays content from Wikipedia for info on certain subjects

* DDG displays content from StackOverflow for programming questions on the side for convenience

* DDG is highly customizable, you can change the theme of text, background, and searchbars to whatever color you want

Cons

* Image search isn't up-to-par compared to Google images

Ecosia

Ecosia is a search engine from Germany similar to DuckDuckGo that along with private searching, donations are made to plant more trees with every search.

Pros

* Searches are private

* Every search builds up to planting more trees in the world, which will help the environment

* Search results are very similar to DuckDuckGo

* You have gotten rid of another Google product from your life

Cons

* None that I can currently find

Google Hangouts alternates

Google Hangouts is Googles messaging service for group conversations. The consumer version of Hangouts is going out of support in June 2020.

Discord

Discord is a high level Voice Over IP (VoIP) service designed for gaming. It is also a good messaging service. You can run and manage your own server(s) and visit other servers. It is a good communication tool, and it is very popular as of February 10th 2020

Pros

* Discord works on most major platforms (Windows, Linux (and Android), MacOS, and iOS)

* Discord has high functionality and allows text messages with images and videos

* Custom emojis are supported

* You can connect games to Discord and play with friends

Cons

* Discord isnt very focused on user privacy

* Discord has some bad (and sometimes illegal) servers

* Some features of Discord cost real money.

ChromeOS alternates

ChromeOS is one of Googles products. It is hardly an operating system, it is mainly just the Google Chrome web browser on top of Debian (Linux) (formerly Gentoo (Linux)) when Degoogling, Googles roots are embedded deep into the system, and it is recommended to just get rid of the Chromebook altogether when Degoogling.

There aren't any web-based operating systems that can be put on a Chromebook, and it wouldn't be degoogling either. What should be done is getting a different laptop that isn't by Google or a Chromebook, and putting Linux, BSD, or another UNIX-based system on it (MacOS or Windows if you don't care about your privacy but hate Google)

There are too many examples of replacement operating systems. All that you need is a computer that is capable of browsing the internet (almost any computer from 1997 or later) and the installer file(s) of a modern browser on it that isn't Google Chrome or Chromium based.
